The term United Kingdom or UK for short, became official in 1801 when the parliaments of Great Britain and Ireland passed an Act of Union.


This united the two kingdoms of Great Britain and Ireland together into the United Kingdom, this union would only last until 1922 when the irish war of independence broke Ireland off from the union.


However the UK is still a thing to this day, the nations that are still apart of this union are;


  • England
  • Wales
  • Scotland
  • Northern Ireland

In many ways you can see the UK as a state system not to distant from that of United States, each member of the UK has their own laws but the UK as a whole also has a set of laws which are followed by its members.
