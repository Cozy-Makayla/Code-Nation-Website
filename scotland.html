Scotland


This is the Scottish flag, also known as Saint Andrew's Cross or the Saltire.


This version of the flag can be traced back to the mid 16th century when it was illustrated in Sir David Lyndsay of the Mount's Register of Scottish Arms, try and say that ten times.


The white cross on blue background form an intergeral part of the United Kingdom Flag.

The name "Scotland" waas first coined in the 9th century as part of the Kingdom of Scotland, but before this time Scotland was known as many different names, the Greek sailor Pytheas refered to the area as "Orcas" which is where the name for the Orkney islands comes from today.


During the Roman's invasion of Britannia, Scotland remained mostly untouched, this however wasn't for lack of trying on the Romans part. One famous incursion by the Romans was in 79AD when Agricola invaded the southern region of modern day Scotland, this however only lasted a few years before the Romans withdrew from the area.
