Wales


This is the Welsh flag, which is known is welsh "Y Ddraig Goch", which translates to 'the red dragon' in english.


The current version of the flag was adopted in 1959, and has several elements from the tudor period such as the green and white colours.

Modern day wales, shares borders with England to it's east and to the west is the Irish sea, because of its location many holiday makers travel to Wales during the summer to camp either in tents or more commonly in carvans both statics and traveling versions.


The early identity of the Welsh people originates from the withdrawal of the Romans, today Wales is considered one of the modern Celtic nations.


Wales was annexed by England first during the 13th century, but later claimed independence, Wales was then annex a second and final time during the 16th century and has been a member of Britain ever since, they still however have their own culture and language native to the country.


During the industrial period in Great Britain Wales was transformed from a agricultural nation to that of a mining and metallurgical powerhouse, this caused wales population to spike during this time period.
